Day 5: Strategy

  • It is ok if you do not get a perfect pass every single time. Many passers get stuck in a loop for perfection and that can lead to overpasses and shanks. Sometimes when facing a tough server it is best to just get the ball in the air and fight it off to the 10 foot line instead of trying to go for the perfect pass.

  • Depending on the type of serve(float or top), many passers change how they pass. On a topspin serve, passers typically start further back because it is much easier to move forward then backwards when passing and topspins typically do not fall short. This also gives the passers a little more time to react to the serve and make a correct read on the ball.

  • When there is a float serve, passers typically start a little but further up since float serves are much more versatile and can be placed anywhere. Many passers also prefer to use their hands when passing a float serve as they do not come as fast as tops and are more easily controlled with their hands.
